Quick Reference

Flexible Meal Templates

Breakfast: Protein + carbs + fruit

Lunch: Lean protein + carb + vegetables + fat

Dinner: Protein + vegetables + carb or fat

Snack: High-protein + fruit or nuts

Sample Day of Eating

Breakfast — Protein Oatmeal Bowl: 3 whole eggs, 1 cup egg whites, 1 cup oats, 1 banana, 1 cup blueberries, cinnamon, water or milk.

Lunch — Lean Chicken Power Bowl: Grilled chicken breast, white rice, broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, avocado, olive oil, salt, pepper.

Snack — Greek Yogurt Bowl: Plain Greek yogurt, strawberries, blueberries, honey, chopped almonds.

Dinner — Salmon Performance Plate: Wild salmon, roasted potatoes, asparagus, mixed greens, olive oil, lemon, salt, pepper.

Evening Snack (optional): Cottage cheese, mixed berries, walnuts.

Food Substitution List

Protein: Chicken → turkey, tuna, lean beef, eggs, Greek yogurt

Carbs: Rice → potatoes, oats, pasta, bread, fruit

Fats: Olive oil → avocado, nuts, salmon, eggs

Snacks: Greek yogurt → cottage cheese, protein shakes

Common Mistake Fixes
  • Fat loss stopped → slightly reduce calories or increase steps
  • Strength dropped → increase protein and recovery
  • Hunger is high → add more volume foods (vegetables, potatoes)
  • Feeling tired → improve sleep and reduce deficit size
Weekend Flexibility Rules

80/20 rule — mostly consistent, some flexibility. One meal won't ruin progress. If you slip, return to normal next meal. No restriction cycles or punishment dieting.

Eating out: prioritize protein first, choose grilled or simple meals, don't stress small extras.
